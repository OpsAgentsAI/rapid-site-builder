## Page hero

**Your site builder. Your choice of keys.**

Run Rapid Site Builder fully managed on our cloud and AI — or bring your own keys and
run the same product on your own Google Cloud project. Same builder, same output,
two ways to own it.

## The two tiers, side by side

### 🏢 Managed — *"You build. We run everything."*

Sign in and start building. Your sites, AI generation, hosting, and monitoring all run
on OpsAgents infrastructure — no cloud account, no API keys, no quotas to manage.

- **Zero setup** — no GCP project, no service accounts, no key rotation. Ever.
- **Predictable** — one subscription covers the AI generation your sites use.
- **Backed by us** — uptime, upgrades, and incident response are our job, with a real SLA.
- **Best for:** businesses that want a website that works, not infrastructure to babysit.

### 🔑 BYOK — *"Your keys. Your cloud. Our software."*

The same Rapid Site Builder, running against **your** Google Cloud project and your own
observability stack. You bring your Vertex AI credentials; AI usage lands on your own
cloud bill, under your own governance.

- **Your data plane** — model calls and generated assets run in your GCP project,
  inside your compliance boundary.
- **Your spend, your rates** — AI usage bills straight to your cloud account, with any
  committed-use discounts you already have.
- **Lightest entry point** — a software license, not a hosting contract.
- **Best for:** teams that already live on Google Cloud and want control first.

## Objection handling (FAQ seeds)

**"Is BYOK open source?"** No. Both tiers run the same proprietary product — with BYOK
you inject your credentials into our software; the source never ships. You license the
builder, you own the keys and the output.

**"Can I switch?"** Yes, both directions. Start BYOK and upgrade to Managed when key
rotation, cloud-bill surprises, or SLA needs outgrow self-managing — or move from
Managed to BYOK when your compliance team wants the data plane in-house. Your sites
come with you either way.

**"Why would I pay more for Managed?"** Because keys are work. Rotation, IAM, quota
requests, regional availability, model deprecations, monitoring — Managed makes all of
that our problem, with an SLA attached.

## Upgrade nudges (in-product, BYOK → Managed)

- On a key/credential failure: *"Tired of rotating keys? Managed runs this for you — SLA included."*
- On a quota error from their project: *"Out of quota in your project. Managed capacity is pre-provisioned."*
- On the third month of variable spend: *"Prefer one predictable line item? See Managed."*
